What lives at /garden — and what lives elsewhere

A garden is a group of people with a shared treasury. /garden/[name] is the management surface — identity, roster, configuration, money. Activity that happens inside a garden — proposals, contributions, conversation, voting — lives at /community/[name]. The two are linked, not nested.

Tab / Section Contains Purpose
Overview Identity, status, mission, recent activity, cross-route into /community The cover page — what is this garden, where does it stand, what's happening lately. Public-readable.
Members Roster (Operators · Reviewers · Gardeners), pending invites, role chips, contribution counts Who's in this garden and what they can do. Operators invite, change roles, revoke.
Settings Name · slug · ENS · subdomain · location · mission · visibility · created · domains · danger zone The configuration record — every editable field of the garden, plus archive / dissolve.
Treasury Balance, holdings (USDC · ETH · SEED), recent distributions, draft proposals Money in / money out + the proposal drafts that haven't been published to /community yet.
Not here → Active proposals · voting · contribution feed · discussion Lives at /community/[name]. Both Overview tabs link there ("Open community →").

Dossier — the chosen direction

Top tabs · flat hairlined surfaces · sticky identity · stats above tabs

A garden read as a public-facing record — identity stated once at the top, a compact status row below it (visibility · cycle · recent contribution / distribution counts), then the operator's working sections behind a top tab rail. The grammar is inherited from Hub: same segmented tabs, same count chips, so an operator who lives in the queue at /hub doesn't relearn navigation here.

Surfaces are flat. Sections sit on the canvas in hairlined cards (raised over outline). The status row is the only differentiated band. The result reads like a printed dossier: facts plainly stated, separated by hairlines, not by depth. Overview is split 2-up + 1-down — mission / status / domains on the left, recent activity on the right, two cross-route cards into /community below.

What you give up: the dossier reads cleanly but feels less alive than B — a working operator opening the page sees a settled record rather than a workspace they can immediately act on. Overview's recent-activity column is a read-only echo of /community's feed, which is intentional but worth noting.

Constraint check

  • 4 tabs — Overview · Members · Settings · Treasury
  • Control heights · 32 / 40 / 48 only
  • Shape scale · 0 / 4 / 8 / 10 / 12 / 14 / 9999
  • Spring motion only — no raw cubic-bezier
  • Glass on AppBar only (sections are flat)
  • ≤ 2 nesting levels under MainSheet
  • One primary affordance per section header
  • No banned vocabulary (dashboard / KPI / leaderboard / hero / streak)
  • Plus Jakarta Sans only · JetBrains Mono for ENS / IDs / amounts
  • Inherits Hub AppBar + NavBar — same chrome, same garden-pill
  • /garden = management · /community = activity (cross-route only)
