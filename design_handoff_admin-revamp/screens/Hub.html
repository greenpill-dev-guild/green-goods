What changed from the first pass · three glass iterations

What changed

  • All 3 trailing icons in the AppBar share one styling (no more avatar circle)
  • Numeric context strip removed — counts already live on the tabs
  • Single green family — pipeline dots, presence dot, tab count + FAB all align to one hue
  • Card click opens an animated RightSheet (320ms spring · scrim fade · Esc to close)
  • Three glass iterations exposed via Tweaks: Solid · Soft glass · Atmospheric
Glass treatment grammar

Solid stays the conservative material baseline — opaque chrome, hairline borders. Soft glass applies Liquid Glass to the filter rail (tabs, search, sort, chips, navbar) while cards stay opaque so titles and metadata read clean. Atmospheric extends the material to the cards themselves and saturates the canvas wash, so each card reads as a glass tile floating over the warm earth backdrop.

Click any card in any variant to see the RightSheet animation. Use the Tweaks panel (toggle in the toolbar) to switch between the three surface treatments.

Variation A — Reference grammar, refined

Axes · filter affordances · density (comfortable)

A faithful evolution of the validated reference. Card composition is preserved exactly — three-photo collage with a 1/N counter, category badge top-left of the photo, a 3-pixel state edge along the top, title plus author·garden, status-dot pipeline, and relative time bottom-right. The two divergences are filter affordances and page header weight.

Filtering moves from a single sort dropdown to a pill chip group for category — Agro · Waste · Solar · Water · Habitat — sitting between the search bar and the sort control. One click slices the queue by Action type, which is the primary mental model an Operator carries between Gardens. Search keeps its capsule and gains a ⌘K affordance, signalling full structured-filter intent without committing to a command palette.

The header replaces the current "Hub workbench" title with a numeric context strip — plain text, no KPI tiles, no decorative chrome: "12 in review · 5 assessing · 3 to certify · Synced 2 minutes ago". Operators get the same situational read in two seconds without the dashboard framing.

What you give up: chip-row eats horizontal real estate, so the third card column is slightly tighter than the reference at 1440. Multi-select state across chip filters has a low ceiling — combining four categories with three statuses needs a richer surface (which Variation B provides).

Variation B — Operator's desk

Axes · card composition (compressed list-row) · filter affordances (left-sheet rail)

A density-first take aimed at the operator who opens Hub at the start of a session and needs to dispatch 30+ items without losing the photo-as-identity grammar. Card composition collapses to a list-row: a 56×48 thumb (with +N count chip when there's a collage), inline category badge, title + author·garden, then pipeline dots and time on the right. The state edge becomes a vertical 3px rail at the row's leading edge — same semantic, smaller footprint.

Filter affordances move into a persistent LeftSheet rail: three sections — Saved (All Work · Assigned to me · Flagged · Older than 24h), Action (multi-select check), Garden (multi-select check). Saved filters give power users one click to a slice they hit daily; check groups handle the multi-axis combinations chip rows can't. Header reduces to title + match count + tabs + thin toolbar — all chrome serves the row list.

What you give up: the photo collage's ambient weight is gone — Work feels more like a queue and less like a gallery of evidence. New Operators may find Variation A easier to learn, but a returning Operator gets through 8 rows where they previously saw 5 cards, and the rail keeps complex slices a single check away.
