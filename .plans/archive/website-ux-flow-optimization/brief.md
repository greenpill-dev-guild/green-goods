# Website UX Flow Optimization

**Slug**: `website-ux-flow-optimization`
**Stage**: `active`
**Priority**: `p1`
**Created**: `2026-05-08`

## Problem

The public website has received several rounds of surface polish, but the visitor flows still have friction at decision points: funding language can become too technical, `/cookies` is hard to discover, success states do not always point visitors to a next step, and loading/empty/error states can still feel silent.

## Desired Outcome

- First-time visitors can understand, find, fund, and follow Garden work without needing web3 vocabulary.
- The seven core visitor flows are reachable and recoverable on desktop and mobile.
- Async states are honest: loading, empty, error, and success each give the visitor a clear explanation or next action.
- The work stays inside the public browser/client experience and does not start a broader PWA/admin redesign.

## Scope Notes

- In scope: public browser flow copy, public funding dialog language, public success wayfinding, visible loading/empty/error states, `/cookies` and `/actions` discovery, first-mention term explanations, targeted i18n.
- Out of scope: wallet disconnect revalidation, distinctive Garden-card state badges, Garden filters by location/domain, return-visitor memory, admin UI, installed PWA shell, contracts, indexer schema work.

## Success Signal

A cold visitor can land on the site and reach each public flow within two clicks, with no critical decision moment relying on unexplained web3 vocabulary and no silent async state.
