# Vrbs x Green Goods: Impact Backed Revenue for Nounish Public Goods

## A Draft for Discussion

Over the past year at the [Greenpill Dev Guild](https://app.charmverse.io/greenpill-dev-guild), we have been building [Green Goods](https://greengoods.app), an impact reporting protocol that makes it simple for local communities to **capture regenerative work onchain**. From tree plantings and community gardens to neighborhood cleanups, the most meaningful impact starts hyperlocal and scales from there.

By connecting verified impact with tokenized revenue through [RevNets](https://rev.eth.sucks/memo/), we can create a model where **funding public goods is not a one way door**. Funders keep skin in the game, communities own their economic upside, and revenue flows back to the DAOs that believed early.

We think [Vrbs](https://vrbs.build/) is the right community to pilot this with. This post outlines what we are proposing, how the revenue model works, and what we would love your input on.

---

## The Core Idea

Today when a DAO funds a public goods project, **the capital leaves and does not come back**. The impact might be real, but the treasury does not benefit from the value created downstream.

Green Goods already has infrastructure to verify regenerative work, generate yield through [Octant](https://octant.build/) vaults, and route that yield to multiple destinations. We are **currently deployed on Arbitrum** with Celo deployment coming next. What we are proposing is to add a [RevNet](https://rev.eth.sucks/) layer that gives communities the ability to **tokenize their goods and services**, connecting those tokens back to Green Goods in a way that generates returns for early funders.

**Vrbs would be the founding pilot partner.** The vision extends beyond one community. As the model proves out, we onboard more public goods communities and create a network effect where each new participant strengthens the whole ecosystem. Vrbs shapes how this works and benefits as the network grows.

In plain terms:
- Vrbs funds the development of this RevNet integration
- Communities launch gardens and create RevNets tied to **specific impact** — olive oil, reforestation, community solar
- Revenue flows back to Green Goods at the protocol level, and from there, **back to Vrbs**

---

## How the Architecture Works

Two layers.

**Protocol level — [Juicebox](https://juicebox.money/) project (governed).** Green Goods operates as a governed Juicebox project with a `$GOODS` token. This is the shared infrastructure — the [app](https://greengoods.app), the verification system, the [indexer](https://docs.greengoods.app/builders/architecture), the contracts. All garden RevNets contribute revenue back here. This is how the protocol sustains itself and how early funders see returns.

**Garden level — [RevNets](https://rev.eth.sucks/) (autonomous).** Each community gets RevNets tied to the specific goods and services they produce. Fully autonomous, rules set at deploy, no governance overhead. The token reflects the value of that specific work over time.

**How Vrbs benefits:**
- All garden RevNets route revenue to the **Green Goods Juicebox**
- Green Goods allocates a portion back to Vrbs — **repayment plus ongoing returns**
- Vrbs accumulates **Green Goods Juicebox tokens**, a stake in the protocol as more gardens come online
- Vrbs receives **RevNet token allocations** from pilot gardens, direct exposure to the impact communities it helped launch
- The exact structure and formula we want to define together before this becomes a formal proposal

We are not hardcoding Vrbs into every garden contract. Vrbs invests at the protocol level and benefits as the **whole ecosystem scales**. A garden in Lagos or São Paulo still flows revenue through Green Goods and back to the funders who made it possible.

---

## Multiple RevNets Per Garden

Gardens are hubs of community and environmental action. **The work they do is diverse, and each distinct good or service can have its own RevNet** with its own token and treasury.

- A community garden might have a RevNet for **seasonal harvest shares** — a blockchain native CSA (community supported agriculture)
- The same garden runs a separate RevNet for **reforestation work**
- Each token carries **strong conviction** because it is tied to specific, verifiable impact rather than a vague general purpose treasury

As we validate which models work — mushroom farming, solar installations, regenerative agriculture — we offer those as **turnkey templates** for new gardens. Green Goods becomes a library of impact backed token models that communities can deploy.

---

## A Network of Public Goods Communities

Vrbs is the pilot, but the ambition is bigger. We want Green Goods to become **the protocol that public goods communities use to tokenize their impact** and generate sustainable revenue.

After Vrbs, we are looking at:
- **[Greenpill](https://greenpill.network/) chapters** across the network
- **ReFi local nodes** already doing regenerative work on the ground
- **Other Nounish communities** aligned with public goods

Each one launches gardens, creates RevNets, and contributes back to the protocol. **The more communities that participate, the more value flows through the system**, and the more Vrbs benefits from having been the founding funder.

---

## Why This Is Closer Than It Sounds

Green Goods is a working protocol with **over 25 smart contracts deployed on Arbitrum**, with Celo coming next. You can try the [app](https://greengoods.app) and explore the [admin dashboard](https://admin.greengoods.app) today.

The piece that makes this low lift is our **YieldResolver**. It already splits yield from Octant vaults three ways:
- **Cookie Jar** — operational compensation for gardeners
- **[Hypercert](https://hypercerts.org/) fractions** — impact certificate purchases
- **[Juicebox](https://juicebox.money/) treasury** — grows token backing

Right now that third destination points to a test contract. **Connecting it to a live RevNet is adding another address to the splitter.**

The verification pipeline exists. The yield pipeline exists. The role management exists. What we need is the RevNet deployment, the revenue routing to Vrbs, and initial capital to demonstrate the flywheel end to end.

## What We Are Asking For

**3 ETH** to build the RevNet integration and launch the first pilot garden.

- **1.5 ETH — RevNet deployment and integration.** Deploy on [Arbitrum](https://arbitrum.io/), build garden templates, connect our existing yield pipeline to the live contracts, configure Juicebox allocation back to Vrbs.
- **1 ETH — App integration and branding.** RevNet management in the [Green Goods app](https://greengoods.app), Vrbs branded pilot garden, community onboarding flow.
- **0.5 ETH — Community growth and outreach.** Community workshops, co-marketing with Vrbs, onboarding materials, and outreach to pilot garden communities.

---

## How This Scales and Pays Back

**Starting out.** As the first gardens launch and supporters deposit into Octant vaults, yield begins generating through [AAVE V3](https://aave.com/) at ~4% annually. Revenue starts flowing back through the protocol to Vrbs from day one.

**As gardens grow.** Supporters buy RevNet tokens directly, treasuries grow faster than yield alone. At **10-30 ETH in total inflows** over the first year or two, protocol revenue flowing back to Vrbs could recoup the initial investment and beyond.

**Network effect.** This is the real opportunity. Each new public goods community that joins adds revenue to the protocol. Vrbs does not need to fund each one individually.
- **5 gardens by year two** — multiple revenue streams
- **20 gardens by year three** — compounding network effects
- Every new garden strengthens the network **Vrbs helped build**

Specific repayment terms — fixed return target, time based, or ongoing percentage — we want to define together before the formal proposal.

## What Vrbs Gets

- **Revenue that compounds** — protocol level investment that generates returns as the ecosystem grows
- **Token upside** — Green Goods Juicebox tokens + RevNet allocations from pilot gardens
- **Real world proliferation** — [Hypercerts](https://hypercerts.org/) and RevNet tokens carrying the **Vrbs brand** on verified regenerative work, Noggles on tree plantings onchain and in the ground
- **First mover advantage** — founding pilot partner that shapes the model and benefits most as it scales

## What We Want to Discuss

This is a draft because we want to **build this with the Vrbs community**, not just for it.

- **What impact resonates most with Vrbs?** We scope the pilot to match what the community cares about.
- **What repayment and upside structure feels right?** Both repayment terms and ongoing token allocations — what makes sense given the risk?
- **Who wants to get involved?** Vrbs members interested in documenting regenerative work or shaping RevNet templates, we want you in early.
- **How aggressive should the tokenomics be?** Conservative (strong floor, slow growth) or aggressive (faster growth, more volatility)?
- **Other communities to bring in?** Know a group that would pilot a garden alongside Vrbs? We plan for it from the start.

We are excited about where this is headed and want to hear how it connects to what Vrbs is building. **Drop a comment, share your thoughts**, and let us figure out the best path forward together.
