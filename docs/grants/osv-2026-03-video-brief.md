# OSV Video Brief

## Goal

Deliver a public or unlisted video under 60 seconds that explains:

- the problem
- who is leading the work
- why Greenpill Dev Guild is the right group to execute it
- what the next 9 months will make real

## Tone

- calm
- clear
- builder-led
- field-grounded
- ambitious without hype

Avoid sounding like a startup ad or a protocol explainer.

## 60-Second Structure

### 0-10 seconds

State the problem in human terms.

Suggested line:

People doing vital ecological and civic work are often invisible because the tools for proving that work are too complex, too online, or built for institutions instead of communities.

### 10-25 seconds

State what Green Goods does.

Suggested line:

Green Goods helps communities document real work with simple mobile, voice, and messaging flows, even in low-connectivity settings, so that work can be trusted, coordinated, and supported.

### 25-40 seconds

State who is building it.

Suggested line:

I lead this work through Greenpill Dev Guild, a public-goods builder collective, and Green Goods is our flagship product for turning invisible labor into usable proof.

### 40-55 seconds

State what the OSV funding makes possible.

Suggested line:

Over the next 9 months, we want to build the pilot-ready system in two months and start a live pilot in month three with communities already doing this work in places like Nigeria and Cape Town.

### 55-60 seconds

End with the bigger reason.

Suggested line:

If this works, communities doing real public goods work can become easier to trust, easier to coordinate, and easier to fund.

## Visual Beats

- Afolabi speaking directly to camera
- screenshots or short clips of Green Goods
- one visual of field work: solar, waste, planting, or similar
- one line naming Greenpill Dev Guild
- closing line with the project thesis

## Recording Notes

- keep the camera steady
- use natural light if possible
- speak slower than feels necessary
- avoid reading a dense script word-for-word
- do not overload the video with protocol terminology
