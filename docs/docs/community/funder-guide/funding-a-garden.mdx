# Funding A Garden

The most reliable funding path in Green Goods today is **depositing into a garden vault**. Start there. Hypercerts matter in the ecosystem, but if you want a clear, repeatable user flow right now, open a garden and fund it through Treasury.

## How Funding Works

Funding in Green Goods currently has two layers:

### Vault Deposits

**Octant Vaults** are impact-vault deposit contracts attached to each garden. When you deposit ERC-20 tokens:

1. Your deposit adds principal to the garden endowment
2. Strategies may generate yield in the background while depositor claim value stays flat by design
3. When harvested, yield is split three ways:
   - **Cookie Jar** — Petty cash for gardeners' daily operations
   - **Hypercert Fractions** — Purchasing impact certificate fractions
   - **Community Endowment** — Long-term community treasury

Your principal stays in the vault, and harvested yield funds garden operations through protocol routing rather than depositor share appreciation. You can withdraw your deposit at any time.

### Hypercert Purchases

**Hypercerts** are tokenized impact certificates that represent verified regenerative work. They are real, and operator-facing mint/list flows exist, but the smoothest funder journey in Green Goods is still the vault path first. Treat direct Hypercert buying as a more advanced path that depends on where a garden is listing and how that market is being surfaced.

- Use Hypercerts when you want claim-level exposure to a specific impact bundle
- Use vaults when you want the clearest current in-product funding flow

## Getting Started

1. **Open a garden you want to support** in the Green Goods app.
2. **Review recent evidence** — check work volume, approvals, assessments, and any treasury activity that helps you judge cadence.
3. **Open Treasury and deposit** the asset and amount you want to commit.
4. **Track the garden over time** — look at approved work, assessment quality, and routed treasury activity, not just the act of depositing.
