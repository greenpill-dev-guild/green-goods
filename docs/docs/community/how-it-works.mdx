# How It Works

Green Goods combines several innovations to make documenting and funding regenerative work practical for field workers. Here's how each piece works.

## Frictionless Onboarding

{/* IMAGE PLACEHOLDER: Passkey onboarding — fingerprint/face auth on phone (800x400) */}

Green Goods eliminates the biggest barrier to web3 adoption — wallet setup:

- **Passkey authentication** — Sign in with your fingerprint, face, or device PIN. No seed phrases, no browser extensions, no MetaMask popups.
- **Automatic smart account** — Behind the scenes, a [smart account](/glossary#smart-account-account-abstraction) is created for you using account abstraction. This is your on-chain identity.
- **Gasless transactions** — Work submissions are bundled and sponsored, so you never need to buy tokens or pay gas fees.
- **Under a minute** — From first visit to first submission, the onboarding flow takes less than 60 seconds.

Experienced users can also connect an existing wallet (MetaMask, Rainbow, etc.) for direct on-chain interaction.

## Documenting Your Work

{/* IMAGE PLACEHOLDER: MDR workflow — three-step phone screens showing Media, Details, Review (800x400) */}

The **Media -> Details -> Review** (MDR) workflow is how every piece of work gets documented. It's designed for mobile phones in the field:

1. **Media** — Open the camera and capture photos of your work. The action specifies minimum and maximum media requirements. Photos are stored locally on your device first.
2. **Details** — Fill in action-specific form fields. These are generated from the action's configuration and can include text inputs, number fields, dropdowns, sliders, and repeating rows.
3. **Review** — See a summary of your photos and answers. Add optional feedback, then submit. The submission enters the operator's review queue.

The MDR flow ensures consistent, structured evidence capture regardless of the action type or domain. Here's the full step-by-step:

{/* IMAGE PLACEHOLDER: Work logging — gardener photographing tree planting (800x400) */}

1. **Open the app** and navigate to your garden
2. **Browse available actions** — Each action card shows the title, domain badge, description, and requirements
3. **Select an action** to start the MDR workflow
4. **Capture media** — Take photos using your device camera or select from your gallery
5. **Fill in details** — Complete the action-specific form fields
6. **Review and submit** — Check your submission summary and tap submit
7. **Track progress** — Monitor your submission status in the work dashboard (pending, approved, or changes requested)

## Built for the Field

Green Goods is a [Progressive Web App](/glossary#pwa-progressive-web-app) — it works like a native app on your phone, even in low-connectivity environments:

- **Install from your browser** — Tap "Add to Home Screen" on any mobile browser. No app store required.
- **Offline-first** — All submissions are saved locally before syncing. The service worker caches the app shell and critical assets.
- **Background sync** — When you come back online, queued submissions sync automatically with exponential backoff and retry logic.
- **Mobile optimized** — Designed for mid-range Android devices with limited bandwidth. Touch targets, image compression, and lazy loading keep the experience fast.
- **Multi-language** — Full UI support in English, Spanish, and Portuguese. Action titles, descriptions, instructions, and form labels are all translated. Language detection follows your device settings, with manual override available.
- **Community translations** — Translation files are open for community contributions, with French and Swahili next on the roadmap.

## On-Chain Verification & Rewards

Every approved piece of work creates a verifiable on-chain record:

- **EAS attestations** — The [Ethereum Attestation Service](/glossary#eas-ethereum-attestation-service) creates permanent, schema-validated attestations for each approved submission
- **Resolver validation** — Smart contract resolvers enforce that attesters have the correct [Hats Protocol](https://www.hatsprotocol.xyz/) role and that the data matches the expected schema
- **Hypercert certification** — Aggregated, assessed work becomes a [Hypercert](/glossary#hypercert) — a tokenized impact certificate that funders can purchase
- **Reputation building** — Your attestation history builds an on-chain reputation that's portable across gardens and platforms
- **Automated impact reporting** — Green Goods integrates with [Karma GAP](https://gap.karmahq.xyz/) to aggregate approved work across actions and reporting periods. Impact data maps to grant milestones, and evaluators can export reports in formats that funders expect. Garden-level dashboards show work volume, approval rates, and capital measurements over time.
