# Create a Garden

<StatusBadge status="Live" />

## What this helps you do

Deploy a brand-new garden in the Green Goods admin so your community can start submitting work against it. This is a one-time setup — you will not redo it once the garden is live.

Creating a garden is a **deployer-level action**. If you are already an operator inside an existing garden, you probably don't need this page. Jump straight to [Approve Work](./reviewing-work) or [Make an Assessment](./making-an-assessment).

:::note Legacy admin vs. new admin
During the Earth Week activation you may be on the **legacy admin** (left rail shows `Dashboard / Gardens / Assessments / Actions / Endowments`). There, the route is `/gardens/create` (with an `s`), and the wizard labels step 2 **Community** instead of **Plan the initial team**. The step order, fields, and deploy action are the same — just the nav and a couple of labels differ. The screenshots in this guide are from the legacy admin because that is what is live today.
:::

## Before you start

Have these ready so you don't bounce around mid-form:

- **Deployer access** in Green Goods admin. If you hit a permissions error on `/garden/create`, ask the team to add you as a deployer.
- A **name** and a short **description** of the garden.
- A **location** (bioregion, city, or community).
- A **banner image** (optional but recommended).
- An **ENS subdomain** idea — the form suggests one from the name, but you can edit it.
- A **domain selection** — Solar, Agro, Education, or Waste. Domains decide which actions gardeners can submit against later.
- A rough list of the **first operators and gardeners** you want on board. You can add more later from Garden Members, but seeding a few at deploy time keeps things coherent.
- A **funded wallet** on the target chain for the deployment transaction.

:::tip
You need to be online to deploy — the form will block submission if you lose connectivity.
:::

## Step-by-step flow

### Step 1: Open the Create Garden form

1. In admin, open **Gardens** in the left sidebar, then click **Create** (or go directly to `/gardens/create` on legacy admin / `/garden/create` on the new canvas admin).
2. If you hit a 404 or a permissions warning, you don't have deployer access yet. Stop here and ask the team.

<img src="/img/screenshots/admin-create-garden.png" alt="Create Garden — step 1 Garden details with name, location, ENS subdomain, and description fields (legacy admin)" style={{maxWidth: '100%', borderRadius: '8px', border: '1px solid var(--ifm-color-emphasis-300)'}} />

> The form opens on **Garden details**. Fill the required fields, pick the garden's domains, then click **Continue**. Step 2 (**Community** on legacy admin / **Plan the initial team** on the new canvas admin) is for operator + gardener planning notes. Step 3 (**Review & deploy**) summarises everything before you sign the deploy transaction.

> The form opens on **Garden details**. You can return to earlier steps from the progress bar at the top.

### Step 2: Fill in Garden details

1. Enter the **Garden name**. The ENS subdomain field auto-suggests from the name — edit it if you want a cleaner handle.
2. Enter the **location**.
3. Add a **description**. Two or three lines is plenty.
4. Attach a **banner image** if you have one.
5. Choose the **joining policy**: open (anyone can join) or restricted (operators approve each gardener).
6. Select the **domains** this garden will run. Pick only what you actually plan to use — adding a domain later is easier than explaining unused ones.
7. Click **Continue**.

### Step 3: Plan the initial team (labelled "Community" on legacy admin)

1. Add the wallet addresses of the **operators** you want to seed the garden with.
2. Add the **gardeners** who will be submitting work from day one.
3. You can leave either list empty and add members later from Garden Members. Planned members are a coordination list, not on-chain role assignments — the form flags this with a reminder on success.
4. Click **Continue**.

### Step 4: Review and deploy

1. Read back the summary: name, slug, description, joining policy, domains, and planned team.
2. If anything is off, click back into the earlier step and fix it — don't edit by memory.
3. When everything is right, click **Deploy garden** and approve the transaction in your wallet.
4. Wait for the transaction to confirm. The wizard stays on this step until it does.

## How to know it worked

- You are redirected back to the garden list (`/garden` on the canvas admin, `/gardens` on legacy admin).
- A success toast appears: *"Garden created — Your garden has been deployed successfully."*
- If you planned any members at step 3, you also see a reminder toast: *"Add planned members next — N planned members were not assigned during deployment. Add them from Garden Members."*
- The new garden appears in the garden list.
- If you open it in the client app, the banner, location, and description all render as expected.

## If something goes wrong

- **404 on `/garden/create` or `/gardens/create`.** You are not a deployer, or you are on the wrong admin build. Contact the platform admin instead of working around it.
- **"You're offline" toast on Deploy.** The form blocks deployment when the network is down. Reconnect and retry — the form keeps your progress.
- **Transaction cancelled.** You cancelled the signature in your wallet. Click **Retry deployment** when you are ready.
- **Transaction failed.** Check the inline error card — it shows the raw wallet message when one is available. Common causes are insufficient gas funds and a wrong chain. Fix the cause and click **Retry deployment**.
- **Name or ENS collision.** Pick a different garden name or edit the ENS subdomain before retrying.
- **"Cannot deploy yet."** The form isn't ready for submission. Go back through the earlier steps and look for fields marked as required that are still empty.

After a successful deploy, finish setup by:

- Adding members from Garden Members (if you planned any at step 3).
- Confirming that metadata and banner render in both admin and the client app before sharing the garden link with your community.

### Behind the scenes

The deploy transaction creates the garden account on-chain, registers it in the Green Goods registry, and seeds it with the domains you chose. Roles (operator, gardener) are assigned separately after deployment, which is why planned members show up as a reminder rather than being auto-assigned.
