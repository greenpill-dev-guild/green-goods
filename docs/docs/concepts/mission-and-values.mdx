# Mission & Values

Green Goods is built by the **Greenpill Dev Guild**, a collective of developers and designers whose mission is to foster positive-sum actions where **impact = profit**.

## What is Green Goods?

**Regenerative Compliance and Local-First Impact Reporting Platform.**

Green Goods captures, verifies, and funds community-led conservation work. It connects field workers documenting environmental actions to the capital and verification systems that make those actions sustainable.

### Core Loop

```
Evidence Capture → Community Verification → Impact Certification → Capital Formation
(Gardener)         (Operator)               (Evaluator)           (Funder)
```

A **Gardener** photographs their tree planting. An **Operator** reviews and approves the work. An **Evaluator** certifies the impact claim. A **Funder** deposits into a vault that rewards verified impact.

## The Regenerative Stack

Green Goods is one of four products in the Greenpill Dev Guild's Regenerative Stack:

| Product | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Green Goods** | Impact capture, verification, and funding for conservation work |
| **OpenClaw** | Agentic coordination layer — AI-assisted meeting-to-action pipelines |
| **RevNet** | Token engineering for regenerative revenue models |
| **Greenpill Network** | Community coordination across local chapters |

Green Goods is the **field operations layer** — the closest product to real-world impact.

## Organizational Values

### 1. Regenerative Principles

Build systems that create more value than they consume. Every feature should contribute to a net-positive cycle: work creates impact, impact attracts funding, funding enables more work.

### 2. Collaborative Innovation

Work in public, share learnings, and build on open standards. Green Goods uses open protocols (EAS, Hats, Hypercerts) rather than proprietary infrastructure.

### 3. Continuous Learning

Iterate based on field feedback. The gardeners in Cape Town and the agroforesters in Brazil are the primary design reviewers — not abstract personas.

### 4. Sustainable Resources

Minimize operational overhead. The platform should be self-sustaining through yield-backed vaults, not dependent on perpetual grant funding.

### 5. Community Engagement

Every protocol decision affects real communities. Feature trade-offs should be evaluated against the 20+ active garden communities, not hypothetical users.

## Services

The Greenpill Dev Guild provides:

- **Platform development** — Building and maintaining the Green Goods protocol
- **Community onboarding** — Helping garden operators set up and manage their communities
- **Impact methodology** — Designing action schemas and assessment frameworks
- **Integration support** — Connecting to funding mechanisms (Hypercerts, Octant, Gardens)
- **Localization** — Adapting the platform for communities across languages and regions
